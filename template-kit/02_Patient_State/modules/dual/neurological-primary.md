## ⚠️ Analytical Defaults (this module)

1. **Symptom onset, duration, and trajectory are as important as current status** — neurological conditions often have distinct acute, recovery, and residual phases; document all three
2. **Vestibular and neurological conditions frequently have multiple plausible causes** — viral, vascular, autoimmune, and stress-mediated mechanisms can overlap; absence of a confirmed cause is common and should be stated clearly, not papered over
3. **Cognitive symptoms in the context of systemic illness may be secondary** — fatigue, anaemia, thyroid dysfunction, and chronic stress all affect cognition; assess for systemic contributors before attributing to a primary neurological cause
4. **B12 deficiency causes neurological symptoms before anaemia appears** — subacute combined degeneration of the cord can occur with normal haematological findings; active B12 is the most sensitive marker for neurological B12 deficiency
5. **Vestibular recovery is non-linear** — BPPV and vestibular neuritis can overlap; recovery may include periods of apparent worsening; document the trajectory carefully
6. **Stress is a neurological variable** — chronic stress affects HPA axis function, sleep quality, and pain perception; it is also associated with functional neurological symptoms and can lower the threshold for migraines and vestibular episodes in susceptible individuals
7. **Medication effects on neurological symptoms must be noted** — many common medications (including some blood pressure medications, antidepressants, and antihistamines) affect vestibular function, cognition, or seizure threshold

## 6. Cross-System Links

**Neurological → Autoimmune:** Some neurological conditions have an autoimmune component — CNS vasculitis, antibody-mediated encephalitis, and demyelinating conditions are examples. If autoimmune markers are elevated or if the neurological picture is atypical, autoimmune workup is warranted. See `overlays/autoimmune-inflammatory.md`.

**Neurological → Haematology (B12):** Subacute combined degeneration from B12 deficiency causes peripheral neuropathy, balance problems, and cognitive changes — often before haematological B12 is clearly abnormal. Active B12 is the most sensitive marker. See `haematology.md`.

**Neurological → Thyroid:** Hypothyroidism causes fatigue, cognitive slowing, and peripheral neuropathy. Hyperthyroidism causes anxiety, tremor, and palpitations. TSH trend should be part of any neurological symptom workup. See `hormonal-reproductive.md`.

**Neurological → Stress:** Chronic stress affects sleep quality, pain thresholds, HPA axis function, and neurological compensation. Recovery from neurological conditions can be slower in sustained high-stress environments. If stress is a documented trigger or modulator in this patient's history, note it explicitly as a clinical variable — not a lifestyle footnote.

**Neurological → Exercise:** Vestibular rehabilitation relies on movement and exercise. Low Hb can limit exercise tolerance and slow rehabilitation. Calibrate rehabilitation against haematological capacity.
